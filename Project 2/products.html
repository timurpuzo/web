Explore Our Bosnian Traditions Our Specialty

Bosnian coffee, known as "bosanska kafa," is a traditional beverage deeply embedded in the culture of Bosnia and Herzegovina. It is prepared using a small pot called a "džezva," where finely ground coffee is combined with water and brought to a boil. The coffee is served in a small cup known as a "fildžan," often accompanied by a piece of sugar or a sweet treat. Unlike regular coffee, it is not stirred after pouring, allowing the grounds to settle at the bottom. The ritual of making and drinking Bosnian coffee is a social activity, symbolizing hospitality and connection among friends and family.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is your best-selling coffee?

Q: What is the primary difference between Arabica and Robusta coffee beans?
A: Arabica beans have a sweeter, softer taste with higher acidity, while Robusta beans have a stronger, more bitter flavor with higher caffeine content.
Q: What distinguishes espresso from regular coffee?
A: Espresso is made by forcing hot water through finely-ground coffee under high pressure, resulting in a rich, concentrated flavor.
